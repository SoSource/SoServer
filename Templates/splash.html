If you could run your own government as easily as using Facebook,

Would you?


Currently, participation in democracy for most means checking a ballot for one of two or three people, every four years. Thousands of years ago, when democracy was created, that was not the case. At that time citizens could vote on the issues themselves. Many centuries later came representative democracy. It was practical; most citizens were too far away or ignorant of issues.

But they didn't have the Internet, they didn't have smartphones and they didn't have the stakes of today.

Today, your legislative government consists of two chambers, the House and the Senate. The conditions have come to be for a third: the Citizenry. A chamber that consists of every voting age citizen and gives them the ability to collectively approve or veto any new law.

At SoVote,

We believe by engaging with our government we are exercising and fulfilling our civic duty as citizens of our community and country.

We believe we can save ourselves from those ignorant or corrupt by claiming our own power for ourselves.

We believe if enough of us gather in one place, we will be heard.

This is a place to:

  • Represent yourself in all levels of government.
  • Debate the merits of any bill or issue.
  • Vote yea or nay on any bill.
  • Write your own bills.

  • Reduce partisan politics by taking power from those who would vote down party lines.
  • Match your voting history to candidates to see who would best represent your will.

  • Write all votes to a decentralized, immutable blockchain for independant backup and verification.
  • Create a SoVote node for each and every locality.
  • Decentralize Democracy.
